Premise


This dashboard and data are based on a 2018 Washington Post Article detailing the rate of unsolved murders in 50 major U.S. cities. You can pull the data here.

While the original article is very well put together and has more specific map data, you can only look at one city at a time. We (the person who introduced this to me and I) were wondering how the cities compared to eachother and if there were any regional trends in the data (e.g. did police in the south solve more murders than in the north). We were also wondering if any cities had experienced significant improvement over time.

Analysis


While I didn't see any robust regional trends, my data analysis does support the Washington Post's conclusion that murders of white people have a higher rate of being solved (ending in an arrest) than those of minorities, especially African-Americans.

Another takeaway from this project is how much the unsolved murder rate may vary city to city. I was surprised by how relatively low New York's unsolved murder rate (39%) was given that its large population may present issues (Note: NYC only has two years of data). This project also puts into perspective how terrible the situation in Chicago is...

Anyways, I hope you enjoy sifting through the data and visualizations. I apologize for the grim subject matter, but I hope this gives context and information that is helpful.
